Interest in IT

Website and software development. Initially, I had a strong interest in visual design coming from a fine art and philosophy background whereby I was readily exploring the growing link between human – technology interaction, ethics, modernity and art. Over time however, this has evolved into a more practical and holistic implementation of not only my design skills, but my knowledge of societal trends and wants and how each of these elements are unavoidably linked.

Overall, I have had little experience in creating technology but have created two business websites (within the hospitality industry) through hosting sites as part of my current employment as manager whereby I am responsible for the social media and online content.

When choosing my University I had two main concerns; a) a university focused on the duality of entry level skills and the importance of connections and b) the availability of resources and current, industry-immersed teaching staff. RMIT resonated because it seemed to collaborate graduate level skills and connections throughout the initial stages of the IT degree such as making a first year unit UCD, but also offers the foundational basics such as java.

After completing my first Undergraduate degree, I struggled to find an environment that allowed me to be creative yet continue to both develop new skills and remain current. Through my studies at RMIT I hope to learn a variety of overarching computing skills, but more specifically that of coding and software development. I want to be able to solve and trouble shoot problems easily whether personal or within a commercial environment and simultaneously learn to develop programs and interfaces that aim for user satisfaction and pleasure. Ultimately, what I want from my current studies is vague – the perks of entering a degree that is constantly, and rapidly evolving – but I know I want to obtain the skills to hopefully enter a career whereby I play a part in creating the systems, interfaces and communicative protocols of computing applications and websites.

Ideal Employment position

The position calls for a practiced user experience (UX), and user interface (UI) designer who is proficient in designing logical interfaces, prototypes and wire frames; providing feedback and explanations to clients and junior UX team members; engaging in heuristic reviews through reports, presentations and recommendations and collaborating with like-minded people. This position pushes the dichotomy between human-computer interaction and interface development, both of which I have a passion. The main appeal however lies in being paid to create an interface that is not only aesthetically pleasing and visually communicative, but also understanding and implementing the psychology of how people interact and perceive said interface.

You are required to have three to five years’ experience in either the UX or design industry (however, a previous position that occupied both elements would be ideal). It asks for previous tertiary education in IT, computer science, cognitive psychology or similarly related fields such as human computer interaction (HCI). The skills and experience required is vast;

  • The ability to create “simple, powerful and efficient interfaces”
  • Strong communication and networking skills
  • A high working knowledge of artificial intelligence (AI), user journeys and wireframing
  • The ability to create lo-fidelity, interactive prototypes
  • Strong knowledge of UCD methodologies and heuristics
  • An overall high work ethic encompassing a strong attention to detail, pragmatism in planning, prioritizing and organizing effectively and leading as an example within the workplace
  • The ability to produce high quality reports, presentations, recommendations and engage in client discussions of projects
  • Thorough and extensive knowledge, understanding and experience of the ‘user experience’ realm

    • My skills and experience are significantly short of those required for this position. Although I have strong communication and networking skills through both my previous study and my workplace environments whereby customer service, vis-à-vis networking is of a top priority I do not have the minimum level of experience required. Nor am I able to create an interactive prototype based off a client example. However, I do have visual design skills to work with a client to produce that of a ‘simple, efficient and powerful interface’. The best way forward is to immerse myself into the industry through both, obviously, my academic study but also potential internships, freelance work and at ‘home’ practice. Its notwithstanding that I should be proficient in UCD heuristics, creating and producing interactive prototypes and hold a strong knowledge of AI and wireframing by the time I complete my IT degree. During these three years, it would be appropriate to develop my designing and coding skills outside a university setting. Developing websites where possible, or working within a team on startup projects and undergoing extra study through online sources such a Lynda.com, GirldevelopIt or CodeMontage.

    Personal Profile

    The overarching theme in these tests signifies that my personality is best suited for a creative, like-minded and growing environment. I have a strong ability to understand and evaluate my colleagues opinions and ideals, even if they don’t reflect my own. With that being said, the tests reflect my natural inclination towards introversion, avoiding confrontation and rigid workplaces. Because I am heavily creative, intuitive and understanding I have the ability to work within multiple work environments – however, I do not thrive within those that are heavily structured and mundane. Rather, as rated a ‘9’ in conscientiousness (described as impulsive and disorganized) I am best suited for an environment that allows freedom in expression and fosters continual growth. This may be difficult within a setting that does not have the capacity for such a person, for those people that need organization. Being a mediator personality type is a double edge sword; understanding multiple perspectives and sympathizing with each individual within a team setting creates a more cohesive team structure but inevitably leads to constant ‘people pleasing’ and not being able to express myself properly. Moreover, these tests indicate that I don’t take criticism well and often internalise it, therefore further reiterating that I am best suited for an environment that is not only small - to avoid multiple different personality types - but also work best when allowed to work independently.

    Overall, when considering a team environment I need to remain vigilant in my ability to please and sympathise with others. There is a fine line between being considerate within a team environment and fostering a healthy level of dissent and discussion.

    The Project

    Overview

    An interactive, ethical Indigenous Australian art gallery app. Similarly designed to ‘Shopify’, the app will convey traditional ‘shopping’ elements but in an aesthetic representative of Aboriginal culture. The aim is to provide autonomy, business and stability for indigenous artists and communities to cultivate the “one mob, one voice” movement. Within the application there will be artists profiles comprising of both the customers native language and the language of the respective artist, a selection of their art in which the consumer can review the story and meaning associated, an interactive messaging platform, a curated gallery where multiple artists work can be seen as a whole and ultimately the ability to add to cart and purchase.
